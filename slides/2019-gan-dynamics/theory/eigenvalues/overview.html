## Convergence Theory
Understanding the gradient vector field: $$ v(\theta, \psi) = \begin{pmatrix} - \nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}(\theta, \psi) \\ \nabla_\psi \mathcal{L}(\theta, \psi) \end{pmatrix} $$
Local convergence of simultaneous and alternating gradient descent determined by eigenvalues of the Jacobian $$ v^\prime(\theta^*, \psi^*) = \begin{pmatrix} - \nabla^2_\theta \mathcal{L}(\theta, \psi) & -\nabla_{\theta,\psi} \mathcal{L}(\theta, \psi) \\ \nabla_{\theta,\psi} \mathcal{L}(\theta, \psi) & \nabla^2_\psi \mathcal{L}(\theta, \psi) \end{pmatrix} $$
